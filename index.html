About Rajyoga

What is Rajyoga Meditation?

Rajyoga Meditation is a simple and powerful form of meditation taught mainly by the Brahma Kumaris.

  • “Raj” = King (self-control)
  • “Yoga” = Connection (with the Supreme)

So, Rajyoga means becoming the ruler of your mind by connecting with the Supreme Soul (God).

Unlike other meditation:

  • No mantras
  • No complicated posture
  • Can be done with open eyes
  • Can be practiced anywhere, anytime

It is a self-awareness meditation where you realize:
“I am a soul (energy), not just a body.”

Core Philosophy

The core philosophy of Rajyoga rests on the understanding that every individual is fundamentally a peaceful and pure spiritual energy (soul). By shifting our awareness from the physical body to this spiritual identity, we can directly commune with the Supreme Source of light, love, and power.

Rajyoga is based on 3 main ideas:

  • 1. I am a Soul

    - A tiny point of spiritual energy in the forehead

  • 2. God is Supreme Soul

    - A powerful, pure source of peace and energy

  • 3. Connection = Charging

    - When you connect, you recharge your mind

This helps you:

  • Control thoughts
  • Reduce stress
  • Improve focus
  • Feel peace and happiness

Step-by-Step Process

Contemplate (सोच / Awareness): This is the starting point of Rajyoga. Here, you gently change your identity from body-consciousness to soul-consciousness.

What you do:

You create thoughts consciously:
“I am not this body… I am a peaceful soul… a point of light.”

What is happening internally:

• Normally, we think: I am a student, dancer, body, name

• In Rajyoga, you shift to: I am a soul using this body

Why this step is important:

• Your thoughts shape your feelings

• If identity changes → mindset changes → life changes

Example (practical):

Before starting your day:

- Instead of: “I have so much work”

- Think: “I am a calm soul, I can handle everything peacefully.”

Concentrate (एकाग्रता / Focus): You know who you are, the next step is to stabilize your mind.

What you do:

• Focus on:

- Peace

- Stillness

- Positive energy

• You can also visualize:

- A soft light in your forehead

- Calm vibrations spreading inside

What is happening internally:

• Mind starts slowing down

• Random thoughts reduce

• Awareness becomes steady

Common mistake:

People try to “stop thoughts”

Instead, guide thoughts gently

Example:

While studying:

- Mind wandering → bring it back gently

- Think: “I am a focused soul… my mind is stable.”

Experience (अनुभव / Feeling): This is where meditation becomes real and powerful.

What you do:

You don’t just think — you feel deeply:

• Peace

• Love

• Happiness

• Power

Especially by connecting with the Supreme Soul (God)

What is happening internally:

• Thoughts → Emotions

• Emotions → Experience

So now:

You are not saying “I am peaceful”

You are actually feeling peace

Analogy:

Like charging a phone:

- Contemplate = plug in

- Concentrate = connect properly

- Experience = battery charging

Example:

Before a performance (like Kathak):

- Feel: “I am confident, powerful, calm”

- This creates real stage presence

Maintain (धारण / Living it): This is the most important and advanced step. Meditation is not just for 10 minutes. It should become your way of living.

What you do:

• Carry the same awareness into:

- Studying

- Dancing

- Talking

- Stress situations

What is happening internally:

• Reactions reduce

• You respond calmly instead of reacting emotionally

Real-life application:

• Someone criticizes you:

- React: anger

- Maintain: “I am a peaceful soul… I stay stable”

• During a busy day:

- Stay centered and focused

Key skill: Detached involvement

• Do everything

• But stay internally peaceful

Benefits of Rajyoga

  • Better concentration (helpful for studies & coding)
  • Emotional balance (great for performers like dance)
  • Increased energy & confidence
  • Inner peace & stress relief
  • Better relationships (positive thinking)

Important Tips

  • Don’t force your mind — gently guide it
  • Consistency > duration
  • Practice daily (even short sessions)
  • Keep thoughts simple & positive
